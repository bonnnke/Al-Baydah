Sharia Law in UAE
Sharia law, also known as Islamic law, is the legal system of the united Arab Emirates (UAE)
and is based on the Quran and the Hadith (sayings and actions of the Prophet Muhammad).
It is applied to all aspects of ife in the UAE, including personal law ,criminal law and commercial law.

Sharia Law also applied to banking and finance in the UAE , as per the central Bank regulations which prohibit interest (Riba),
and thus most of the banking and financial products are structured in a way that complies with sharia law principles.
In conclusion , the UAE has a dual legal system that applies both civil and sharia law. Sharia law is based on the principles of Islamic law, personal law,
criminal law, and commercial law.
It is important to note that the application and interpretation of sharia law may vary depending on the specific facts of the case and the interpretation.
